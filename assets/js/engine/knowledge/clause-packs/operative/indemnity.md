# Indemnity Clause

## Purpose

Allocate financial responsibility for specified losses, claims, liabilities,
damages, and expenses between contracting parties.

Unlike a standard breach remedy, an indemnity reallocates risk by requiring one
party to protect another from defined legal or financial consequences,
particularly third-party claims.

# Missing Component Risks

Missing Trigger

Risk

Ambiguous scope of indemnity.

Recommendation

Define precise triggering events.

---

Missing Defense

Risk

Uncertainty over litigation responsibility.

Recommendation

Specify duty and control of defense.

---

Missing Settlement Control

Risk

Unauthorized settlements may increase liability.

Recommendation

Require written approval before settlement.

---

Missing Notice

Risk

Late notification disputes.

Recommendation

Define notice procedure and timelines.

---

Missing Exceptions

Risk

Overly broad indemnity exposure.

Recommendation

Define clear carve-outs.

---

Missing Survival

Risk

Indemnity may terminate with the agreement.

Recommendation

State explicit survival period.

---

Missing Liability Cap Interaction

Risk

Conflict with limitation of liability.

Recommendation

Clarify whether indemnity is capped or excluded.

# Jurisdiction Notes

## US

Broad drafting.

Frequently includes duty to defend and hold harmless.

Often heavily negotiated around liability caps.

---

## UK

Generally narrower interpretation.

Third-party indemnities are more common than first-party indemnities.

---

## India

Frequently linked to statutory obligations and Contract Act principles.

Commercial drafting often uses explicit wording to reduce ambiguity.

---

## Australia

Plain-English drafting.

Greater emphasis on proportional liability and insurance.

---

## EU

Often influenced by consumer protection and mandatory national laws.
