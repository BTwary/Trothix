# Limitation of Liability Clause

## Purpose

Allocate and limit the maximum financial exposure of one or both parties by
restricting recoverable damages, excluding certain categories of loss, and
establishing predictable commercial risk.

Unlike indemnity, which transfers liability, a limitation of liability clause
defines the financial boundaries of liability.

# Missing Component Risks

Missing Liability Cap

Risk

Unlimited financial exposure.

Recommendation

Define a maximum liability amount.

---

Missing Damage Exclusions

Risk

Broad damage recovery.

Recommendation

Exclude indirect and consequential damages where appropriate.

---

Missing Carve-Outs

Risk

Critical obligations may become unintentionally capped.

Recommendation

Expressly identify uncapped obligations.

---

Missing Aggregate Cap

Risk

Repeated claims may exceed intended exposure.

Recommendation

Specify aggregate liability.

---

Missing Exclusive Remedy

Risk

Multiple overlapping remedies.

Recommendation

Clarify whether remedies are exclusive.

# Jurisdiction Notes

## US

Frequently excludes consequential, incidental, special, and punitive damages.

Fee-based liability caps are common.

---

## UK

Requires careful drafting of consequential loss due to established case law.

Often expressly lists excluded losses.

---

## India

Courts examine reasonableness and enforceability under the Contract Act.

Mandatory statutory rights cannot always be excluded.

---

## Australia

Plain-English drafting.

Greater attention to consumer protections and proportionate liability.

---

## EU

Mandatory consumer and statutory protections may override contractual caps.

# Cross-Clause Relationships

## Indemnity

Determines whether indemnity obligations are capped, uncapped, or subject to a separate cap.

---

## Warranties

Limits damages recoverable for warranty breaches.

---

## Intellectual Property

IP infringement is commonly carved out from liability caps.

---

## Confidentiality

Confidentiality breaches are frequently treated as uncapped or subject to a super cap.

---

## Data Protection

Privacy obligations often receive separate liability treatment.

---

## Insurance

Insurance limits should align with contractual liability caps.

---

## Payment

Payment obligations are usually excluded from liability limitations.

---

## Force Majeure

Force majeure excuses performance rather than limiting liability, but both clauses shape overall risk allocation.
